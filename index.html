Open Data Platform

DermDB is designed to be a research platform for hosting and storing
data related to skin cancer and other skin related illnesses

  • Take me to the source!

What is DermDB?

DermDB is an open image repository platform for hosting and sharing consumer & research data to analyse skin conditions like cancers & moles.
The initial idea was based off an application that analysed the progression of moles, but we have tailored this now to be as open as possible in servicing many different skin conditions, and accepting input from a multitude of applications, platforms anddevices.

Where next?

One direction we hope to take this project, is to service countries where access to digital devices and media is available more than healthcare may be, such as potential rapid diagnosis of the Zika virus in Brazil. We want to tailor our work on this project in order to benefit as many people as possible, but prioritising those that could benefit the most from it.

Built by DS Cubed Ltd.

About

Research

The anonymised data collected by the application, if authorised by the user should be mineable by researchers to run algorithms and machine learning techniques, alongside our own machine learning algorithms, to come up with possible diagnoses for the user. It then becomes an open and educational data platform, anonymising patients, while empowering them and researchers at the same time.

Developed at HALE 2016

HALE is the Hackathon for Health, Assisted Living & Social Care in Leicestershire organised by DS Cubed Ltd.
